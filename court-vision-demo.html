⚡ RED FLAGS & DELAY BREAKDOWN
SUB BIDS — Day 23
Electrical subcontractor went dark 3 days past bid deadline. Sky escalated directly to the GC within 24 hours. A pre-qualified backup was activated immediately and the original sub resubmitted within 48 hours.
Impact: +0 days — contained with no timeline effect
PERMITTING — Day 45
City plan examiner returned drawings for HVAC load calculation revision — heat gain values did not meet city energy code requirements. Architect resubmitted corrected drawings with updated MEP engineer calculations within 8 business days.
Impact: +17 days — primary delay event on this project
FINAL INSPECTION — Day 91
Inspector flagged that HVAC equipment installed on site did not match the model number specified in the permitted engineering drawings. A field substitution was made during construction without an approved plan revision — triggering a mandatory re-inspection after record drawings were corrected.
Impact: +7 days — re-inspection passed on first attempt
💡 RECOMMENDATIONS — LESSONS LEARNED
01
SCHEDULE A PRE-PERMIT MEETING WITH THE CITY
A 30-minute pre-application meeting with the plan examiner before formal submission eliminates the most common rejection triggers — HVAC load calculations, occupancy classification errors, and exit path compliance. On this project, that meeting would have saved 12–17 days and approximately $4,200 in carrying cost. Sky flags this meeting as a mandatory step on every future permit submission.
02
EQUIPMENT SUBSTITUTION MUST TRIGGER RECORD DRAWING UPDATE
Any field substitution of permitted equipment — HVAC, electrical panels, plumbing fixtures — must trigger an automatic update to the record drawings before final inspection is scheduled. This single protocol eliminates re-inspection delays on virtually every project. Sky now enforces this as a change order checkpoint: no equipment substitution closes without a confirmed record drawing revision.
03
MAINTAIN A PRE-QUALIFIED BACKUP FOR HIGH-RISK TRADES
For electrical, HVAC, and structural trades — the three most common bid delays — maintaining a pre-qualified backup sub means any no-response past deadline can be activated within 24 hours instead of waiting on a single vendor. The sub bid delay on Day 23 was neutralized because a backup was already on file. Every project going forward starts with a backup trade list locked in at pre-con.
